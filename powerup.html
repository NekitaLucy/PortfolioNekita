Using mood boards to brainstorm ideas on the design and testing user preferences between two main styles helped me hone in on my current design for the productivity app

About Power-Up

Level up your productivity and develop positive habits.

The user-friendly habit tracker app, Power-Up, incorporates a unique feature offering three distinct productivity levels. This design allows users to seamlessly balance their to-do lists and add new habits, fostering a balanced and productive lifestyle.

Purpose

To design the UI for the mobile version of a productivity app primarily for 16 to 24-year-olds who want to keep their study plans and to-do lists in one place and manage them on the go.

Objective

Main functionalities

  • Create a user profile
  • Create task lists
  • Mark tasks as complete

Develop 2 to 3 key functionalities and design the UI for their mobile app.
  1. Data management
  2. Incentives

Role

  • Research
  • Ideation
  • Design

Duration of the project

November 2022 - December 2022

Process

Analyze the design of 2 competitor apps.

Competitor Analysis

  1. Habitify
  2. Todoist

I was able to find similar functionalities found in productivity apps.

  • The left tab organizes the primary tasks/habits and the center layout (the main page).
  • A plus button to add new habits or tasks.
  • A calendar.

Features that I applied to design a productivity app.

  • Data management to organize and display the user's progress.
  • A calendar overview
  • The incentive feature when you have completed your goals for the month.
  • Feedback: (rewards notification)

My solution

I chose this idea because I wanted to design a productivity app that lets the user select a limit on how many habits and tasks they want to work on and to motivate the user's goals through an incentive feature.
